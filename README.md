<p class=MsoNormal><b>Introduction<o:p></o:p></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal>This study examines how annual members, and casual riders
use <span class=SpellE>Cyclistic</span> bikes, aiming to uncover patterns that
can inform strategies to convert casual users into subscribed members. The
analysis is based on data from over 5,800 bikes and 600 docking stations across
multiple locations, covering the period from 2019 to 2020. It focuses on ride
durations across different times of day and user types, with inaccurate data
removed to maintain quality. By comparing behaviours across time periods, the
study highlights key differences that can support membership growth.</p>

<p class=MsoNormal><o:p>&nbsp;</o:p></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span class=SpellE><b>Cyclistic</b></span><b> Business Model
Overview<o:p></o:p></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal>Since 2016, <span class=SpellE>Cyclistic’s</span> mission
has been to support public transport users by providing adaptable bikes through
its bike share program. Options include reclining bikes, hand tricycles, and
cargo bikes, designed for people with disabilities and those who do not use
standard two-wheeled bikes.</p>

<p class=MsoNormal>Commuting used to be enjoyable. <span class=SpellE>Cyclistic</span>
aims to bring that experience back by helping users reconnect with city spaces.
Whether commuting or riding for leisure, our membership options meet a range of
needs.</p>

<p class=MsoNormal>Notice your surroundings. You might see a new side of a
building you've passed before. Ever thought, “How have I never seen this park
on my way to work?” Or maybe you stop to look at a flower along the path. Move,
get outdoors, connect, and enjoy the ride.</p>

<p class=MsoNormal><b>Key Tasks<o:p></o:p></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal>The main task for the business is to gain a clear
understanding of how to grow its membership customer portfolio. This aims to
increase profits for operations, reinvestment, or expansion. Converting more
customers to annual members requires defining usage habits and customer
profiles to build a clear target for action. This comes down to knowing your
customer better to offer services that match their needs.</p>

<p class=MsoNormal>*The <i>conflicted</i> package in R helps resolve function
name conflicts that arise when multiple packages (like <i>stats</i>, <span
class=SpellE><i>dplyr</i></span>, or <i>MASS</i>) contain functions with the
same names, such as <i>filter</i>, <i>select</i>, or <i>lag</i>. By requiring
users to explicitly specify which version of a function to use in the event of
a conflict, “conflicted” ensures code clarity, robustness, and reproducibility.</p>

<p class=MsoNormal>The lines <span class=SpellE><i>conflict_<span class=GramE>prefer</span></i></span><span
class=GramE><i>(</i></span><i>&quot;filter&quot;, &quot;<span class=SpellE>dplyr</span>&quot;)</i>

class=GramE><i>(</i></span><i>&quot;lag&quot;, &quot;<span class=SpellE>dplyr</span>&quot;)</i>
instruct R to always use the <span class=SpellE><i>dplyr</i></span> versions of
<span class=GramE><i>filter(</i></span><i>)</i> and <span class=GramE><i>lag(</i></span><i>)</i>
in case of naming conflicts. This is important because functions like <span
class=GramE><i>filter(</i></span><i>)</i> and <span class=GramE><i>lag(</i></span><i>)</i>
also exist in the <i>stats</i> package, where they serve different purposes – such
as signal processing – potentially leading to confusion or errors in data
manipulation tasks.</p>

<p class=MsoNormal><b>Data Insights for Converting Casual Users into Subscribed
Members<o:p></o:p></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><b>1. Casual User Share is Low, But Ride Duration is High<o:p></o:p></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal>Casual users represent 8.61% of all riders. Subscribed
members make up 91.39%.<br>
Casual users account for 38.9% of total ride time, suggesting longer average
ride durations.</p>

<p class=MsoNormal>Opportunity: Offer plans that support longer trips (e.g.
monthly rates, capped long-duration rides) to convert casual users.</p>

<p class=MsoNormal><o:p>&nbsp;</o:p></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><b>2. Member Usage Peaks Align with Commute Hours<o:p></o:p></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal>Member rides peak at 8 AM, 12 PM, and 5 PM.</p>

<ul style='margin-top:0cm' type=disc>
 <li class=MsoNormal style='mso-list:l25 level1 lfo23;tab-stops:list 36.0pt'>8
     AM: 10.92% of member rides</li>
 <li class=MsoNormal style='mso-list:l25 level1 lfo23;tab-stops:list 36.0pt'>12
     PM: 4.66%</li>
 <li class=MsoNormal style='mso-list:l25 level1 lfo23;tab-stops:list 36.0pt'>5
     PM: 13.30%</li>
</ul>

<p class=MsoNormal>Opportunity: Target casual users who ride at similar times.
They may be commuting and open to membership.</p>

<p class=MsoNormal><o:p>&nbsp;</o:p></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><b>3. Casual Rides Peak in Midday Periods<o:p></o:p></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal>Casual rider peak is at 3 PM, making up 11.51% of their
rides.</p>

<p class=MsoNormal>Opportunity: Trial passes (e.g. weekend, short-term) may
appeal to tourists, students, or non-standard workers.</p>

<p class=MsoNormal><o:p>&nbsp;</o:p></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><b>4. Casual Users Log Longer Rides<o:p></o:p></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal>Members ride more often. Casual users ride for longer
durations.<br>
8.61% of users contribute 38.9% of total ride time.</p>

<p class=MsoNormal>Opportunity: Promote plans that lower costs for long trips
(e.g. capped duration, unlimited short rides).</p>

<p class=MsoNormal><o:p>&nbsp;</o:p></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><b>5. January Trends Suggest Fewer Student Riders<o:p></o:p></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal>No increase in ridership during the January break in the
U.S.</p>

<p class=MsoNormal>Opportunity: Consider student-specific offers. Review legal,
insurance, and financial impacts if changing age access rules.</p>

<p class=MsoNormal><o:p>&nbsp;</o:p></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><b>6. Low Night Usage Suggests Operational Window<o:p></o:p></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal>Nighttime ridership is low.</p>

<p class=MsoNormal>Operational Insight: Use night hours for maintenance to
ensure full availability during high-demand periods.</p>

<p class=MsoNormal><o:p>&nbsp;</o:p></p>

<p class=MsoNormal>Insight: A small group of casual users contributes a large
share of ride time. This group holds potential for conversion.</p>

<p class=MsoNormal><o:p>&nbsp;</o:p></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><b>8. Casual Rides Rose in 2020 vs. 2019<o:p></o:p></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal>Casual rides increased in 2020 despite disruptions. This may
reflect changes in transport preference, remote work, tourism decline, or a
preference for socially distanced travel during the pandemic.</p>

<p class=MsoNormal>Opportunity: Review factors behind this rise. Develop
targeted offers to retain these users and convert them to members.</p>

<p class=MsoNormal><o:p>&nbsp;</o:p></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><b>Strategic Recommendations<o:p></o:p></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><b>1. Develop Memberships for Long Trips<o:p></o:p></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal>Design plans for riders with longer durations. This could
improve conversion rates.</p>

<p class=MsoNormal><b>2. Trial Memberships<o:p></o:p></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal>Offer 1-week or 1-month trials to casual users, especially
tourists and students.</p>

<p class=MsoNormal><b>3. Target Off-Peak Riders<o:p></o:p></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal>Use ads or app messages during 3 PM–6 PM to promote
membership.</p>

<p class=MsoNormal><b>4. Focus on Students<o:p></o:p></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal>Offer academic-year plans or back-to-school discounts.
Review compliance with legal standards.</p>

<p class=MsoNormal><b>5. Improve Onboarding<o:p></o:p></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal>Prompt casual users who ride repeatedly or take long trips
with a simple message: “Save more with a membership.”</p>
